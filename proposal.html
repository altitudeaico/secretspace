"I should be working smart. I'm just working hard."

That's what Kam said on our call. It stuck with us — because it perfectly describes the problem we're here to solve. Every booking at Secret Space involves the same sequence of manual steps: read the message, check the calendar, work out the price, write the reply, generate the invoice, tailor the contract, chase the deposit. Each one takes time. Together they take hours.

This system takes all of that off Kam's plate. From the moment an enquiry arrives, to the moment a booking is confirmed — contract signed, deposit received, calendar updated, invoice sent — it happens automatically. Kam gets a notification. The work is done.

How It Works — Part 1

From enquiry to confirmed booking — automatically

Every booking moves through five stages. The system handles each transition. Kam only steps in when something genuinely needs his judgement — which, for most bookings, is rarely or never.

1
Enquiry arrives
Message lands from Instagram, WhatsApp, or email. The system reads it immediately and understands what's being asked.
2
Reply sent
A reply goes back to the client — usually within seconds. It sounds like Kam because it's learned from his past messages.
3
Requirements collected
The client fills in a short form — which room, what sound setup, catering, security. No back and forth.
4
Deposit paid
The client pays securely online. The contract is generated and sent for signing at the same moment.
5
Fully confirmed
Both parties have signed. Calendar updated. Invoice sent. Booking complete. Done.
For a standard booking — how much does Kam do?

For a typical corporate evening, birthday party, or networking event: one tap to approve the reply, or nothing at all if the system is confident enough to send it automatically.

For something unusual — a request type the system hasn't seen before — it holds the reply and asks Kam to review it first. This takes seconds. After that, it handles everything else.

How It Works — Part 2

Replies that sound like Kam — written in seconds

When a message comes in, the system doesn't send a generic acknowledgement. It reads the message, figures out what's being asked, checks the calendar, looks up the right price, and writes a reply using the same tone and language Kam uses himself.

It knows how to do this because we train it on Kam's past conversations before we go live — his WhatsApp messages, his email replies, the way he phrases things with different types of clients. The result is replies that feel personal and natural, not automated.

Every draft shows Kam what the system understood, which rate it applied, and how confident it is. If confidence is high, it sends. If not, it waits for approval. That threshold — how confident the system needs to be before sending automatically — can be adjusted at any time.

The sound setup question

Kam mentioned that every event currently involves a call to the sound engineer to confirm the right setup. With three standard options — Low (background music), Mid (DJ or live band), and High (full festival production) — the client selects what they need at the point of booking, and the sound team are briefed automatically. No consultation needed for standard events.

How It Works — Part 3

Requirements, collected once — properly

Once a client says yes, the system sends them a short requirements form. They pick their options, the system prices it in real time, and everything is locked in without a single back-and-forth message.

Space
Full Venue, Main Hall, Mezzanine, or VIP Suite
Event type
Corporate, private party, networking, creative shoot, dance event, or other
Sound
Low — background music and speeches. Mid — DJ or live band. High — full festival production.
Security
1 guard included as standard. 2 guards for larger events. Client-provided option available.
Catering
Internal approved caterer, external caterer (with insurance form sent automatically), bar only, or dry hire.

If a client selects an external caterer, the Public Liability Insurance form is sent to them automatically the moment they make that selection. Kam doesn't need to remember to ask for it.

How It Works — Part 4

Contracts and invoices — generated and sent without lifting a finger

The moment a deposit is paid, the system generates the contract from the booking details, selects the right template, populates every field, and sends it to the client for signing. Kam countersigns with one tap. The invoice goes out the same moment.

Contract templates
Standard hire, corporate, multi-day, and creative shoot — the right one is chosen automatically based on the booking
E-signature
Sent to the client via their preferred channel (Instagram, WhatsApp, or email) for digital signing
Invoice
Generated from the booking details and sent on confirmation — no manual input
Deposit chase
Automated reminder if deposit isn't paid within 48 hours. Balance reminder 14 days before the event.
Google Calendar
Updated the moment the deposit clears — with all booking details. No manual entry ever.
How It Works — Part 5

The system learns Secret Space — and remembers everything

Before we go live, we train the system on how Secret Space works. Not from scratch — from Kam's real history. The rates, the reply style, the contracts, the way he handles different types of clients. All of it goes in. After that, it runs on autopilot and gets sharper over time.

Kam has a view of everything the system knows — the rate card, the response library, the contracts, the documents it sends automatically. It's transparent, not a black box. If something isn't right, it can be updated in minutes.

Profitability — finally visible

Kam mentioned keeping an hours log in Excel and having a sense that some events take a lot more resource than the revenue suggests. By pulling in his past invoices and that hours log, the system can show the actual margin per event type — not just revenue, but what's left after staff time.

That kind of insight changes how you price and which enquiries you prioritise. Most venue owners never have time to work this out. The system surfaces it automatically.

How It Works — Part 6

What the system doesn't replace

Not everything should be automated. Some things need Kam's presence, his relationships, or his judgement. The system is designed to take away the admin overhead so that Kam can give more energy to the things that actually need him.

Event day management
Staff check-in, on-site logistics, and anything that happens during the event. Kam's team handle this.
Complex negotiations
Large-scale events, unusual requests, multi-event deals — the system flags these for Kam to handle directly.
Key relationships
Returning clients and important relationships stay personal. The system handles the admin; Kam handles the relationship.
Instagram content
Kam asked for tool recommendations rather than a custom build. We'll send a short list of the best off-the-shelf tools — no extra charge.
