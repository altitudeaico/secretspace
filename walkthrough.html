The Problem

The work that's eating your time

Every booking at Secret Space follows the same pattern. A message comes in. You read it, work out the price, write a reply, send an invoice, tailor the contract, chase the deposit, update the calendar. Each step takes a few minutes. Together they take the better part of an hour — for every single booking.

"I should be working smart. I'm just working hard."
Kam — Secret Space

That's the problem this system solves. Not by replacing Kam's judgement or his relationships — but by removing the repetitive admin layer that sits between an enquiry arriving and a booking being confirmed.

Once this is live, a client sends a message. By the time Kam glances at his phone, the reply has already gone, the requirements have been collected, the contract is out for signing, and the calendar has been updated. Kam's involvement, for a typical booking, is a single tap — or nothing at all.

The goal in one sentence
From the moment an enquiry arrives to the moment a booking is confirmed — calendar updated, contract signed, deposit received, invoice sent — the system handles it. Kam steps in only when something genuinely needs his attention.

The AI Reply

Replies that sound like Kam, sent in seconds

When a message comes in, the system doesn't send a generic acknowledgement. It reads the message, understands what's being asked, checks the calendar, looks up the right price, and writes a reply using the same tone Kam uses himself.

It's able to do this because before we go live, we train it on Kam's past conversations — his WhatsApp messages, email replies, the way he phrases things with different types of clients. Corporate enquiries get a professional, direct reply. Birthday party enquiries get something warmer. The system learns the difference.

The enquiry detail view. The system shows exactly what it understood, the rate it applied, and the draft reply. Kam sends as-is, edits, or regenerates — in seconds.

Every draft shows a confidence score. When confidence is high — 85% or above — the reply sends automatically without Kam touching it. When it's lower, the system holds the reply and asks Kam to review it first. That threshold can be adjusted to whatever level of control feels right.

How Kam's time changes

For a standard enquiry — corporate evening, birthday party, networking event — Kam's involvement is one tap to approve, or nothing at all if the system sends automatically.

For an unusual request, the system flags it. Kam reviews in seconds. After that, everything else happens without him.

The Knowledge Base

The system learns Secret Space — and remembers everything

Before we go live, we train the system on how Secret Space actually works. Not from scratch — from Kam's real history. The rates, the reply style, the contracts, the way he handles different types of enquiries. All of it goes in. After that, it runs on that knowledge and gets sharper over time.

Kam can see everything the system knows at any point — the rate card, the response library, the contract templates, the documents it sends automatically. If something isn't right, it can be updated in minutes. It's transparent, not a black box.

Profitability — finally visible

Most venue owners track revenue. Very few track true margin — because accounting for staff hours, setup time, and variable costs against each booking is time-consuming work nobody ever gets around to.

By pulling in Kam's past invoices and the hours log he keeps in Excel, the system calculates the actual margin per event type. That kind of insight changes how you price and what enquiries you prioritise. Salsa and dance events may look healthy on revenue but far less so once hours are factored in. Corporate evenings may be the opposite.

What Stays Manual

What the system doesn't replace

Not everything should be automated. Some things need Kam's presence, his judgement, and his relationships. The system is designed to remove the admin layer so Kam can give more energy to the things that actually need him.

Event day management
Staff check-in, on-site logistics, anything that happens during the event. Kam and his team handle this.
Complex negotiations
Large-scale events, unusual requests, multi-event deals. The system flags these for Kam to handle personally.
Key client relationships
Returning clients and important bookings stay personal. The system handles the admin; Kam handles the relationship.

Delivery

Four weeks, from setup to live

Kam's involvement is concentrated in Week 1. After that, Altitude AI handles the build and checks in regularly. By Week 4, the system is live and Kam has a full video walkthrough to refer back to.

Week 1
Setup and knowledge base
30-minute session with Kam. All documents and data collected. Rate card entered. AI trained on Kam's real messages, contracts, and invoices. Sound options defined. Knowledge base ready to go.
Week 2
Core system build
AI enquiry engine built. Auto-reply, classification, and pricing working. Pipeline dashboard live. Requirements form built and connected. All pieces talking to each other.
Week 3
Connections and integrations
